CSI Cable Connection Guide

Pi Zero 2W + Arducam IMX219 8MP Camera

⚠️

Before you start: The CSI ribbon cable has two distinct sides — a shiny metal contacts side and a plain white / blue stiffener side. Which side faces which direction matters at both connectors. Getting it wrong won't damage anything, but the camera won't be detected.

1

Know Your Cable — Two Sides

CONTACTS side — shiny metal traces visible, printed text ("AWM 20624 80C 60V")
PLAIN side — smooth white plastic, blue stiffener tab at the end
💡
The cable is symmetrical end-to-end — either physical end can go into the Pi or the camera. But you must get the contacts-vs-plain orientation correct at each connector.
2

Connect Cable to Pi Zero 2W

Pi-side orientation

  • Locate the CSI connector — small white clip on the right edge of the board (when USB ports face you at the bottom).
  • Lift the latch — gently pull the dark plastic tab upward with your fingernail. It hinges open ~90°. Do NOT pull it off.
  • Slide cable in — metal contacts facing DOWN toward the PCB (plain/blue side faces up, toward you).
  • Push latch down — press the dark tab back flat to lock the cable in place.
  • Tug test — gently pull the cable. It should not slide out.
Back of Pi Zero 2W — for reference only. Cable connects on the front (component) side.
Which side of cable faces... Direction
Metal contacts (shiny, text) ⬇ DOWN — toward the PCB / board
Plain / blue stiffener ⬆ UP — toward you / away from board
❌
Common mistake: Inserting with contacts facing UP. If rpicam-hello says "no cameras available", flip the cable and retry.
3

Connect Cable to Arducam IMX219

Camera-side orientation

  • Flip the camera over — the CSI connector is on the back of the board (opposite the lens).
  • Lift the latch — same as the Pi: gently pull the dark/white plastic tab upward.
  • Slide cable in — metal contacts facing TOWARD THE BOARD (i.e., facing the back PCB surface, away from you as you look at the back).
  • Another way to think about it: contacts face AWAY from the lens.
  • Push latch down to lock.
  • Tug test — gently pull. Should be secure.
Front of Arducam (lens side) — cable does NOT connect here. Flip to the back.
Which side of cable faces... Direction
Metal contacts (shiny, text) ⬇ DOWN — toward the camera PCB (away from lens)
Plain / blue stiffener ⬆ UP — toward you / toward the lens side
✅
Rule of thumb for both connectors: Metal contacts always face the board. If you can see the shiny traces while the cable is inserted, it's wrong.

Troubleshooting

SymptomFix
rpicam-hello: "no cameras available" Cable orientation is wrong at one or both ends. Power off, flip the cable so contacts face the board, retry.
Camera detected but image is garbled Cable not fully seated. Re-open latch, push cable in further, re-lock.
Intermittent detection Latch not fully closed, or cable is kinked. Reseat and ensure latch clicks flat.
Cable won't fit in connector Make sure you're using the 22-pin Zero ribbon (included with Arducam), not a standard 15-pin Pi ribbon.
